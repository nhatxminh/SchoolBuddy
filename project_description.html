Project Description

1. Overview

Topic:

Acknowledging the rising demand for education and the flaws in our education system nowadays with the need for further improvement in the school management system. We, the School Buddy team have decided to produce an app/web where our users can have a more personalized learning experience.

Supposing that communication is one of the keys to many great accomplishments. School Buddy put great efforts into building a platform where our users can communicate easily from one to another, for example, teachers to parents and teachers to students. Each student’s strengths and weaknesses will be shown throughout their performance on the tests and quizzes. Based on that, teachers can have different approaches to different students, to help improve their weak points and maintain their strengths.

Motivation:

This project draws our attention, mostly because we were still in our educational phase, and we can easily be in the user’s shoes to know exactly what needs improving. making it easier to point out the flaws and what should be changed from the user's perspective, specifically from the student's perspective. In addition to the IT trends nowadays, everything is becoming more and more digitalized than ever. Because of this, we are inspired to work on a web, assisting students to perform at their best without being too stressed out (by focusing only on improving their weaknesses), teachers can teach more effectively, and parents can always see how their child did at school. This project would show future employers that they are investing in making education easier and boosting performance, hence the phrase “Work Smarter Not Harder”.

    2. Detailed Description

    Aims:

    The purpose of this project is to create a mainstream student learning and managing system which is as popular as canvas and blackboard. With a friendly interface, this application is suitable for all users, including parents, teachers, and students. For teachers and parents, our duty is to ensure they can receive the latest and most accurate status occurring in the educational courses. This application not only helps teachers, but also parents to access the timetable and course information of their students. Extra information such as tuition fee reminders and academic status will be sent to parents through the inbox section. Besides, Teachers will be helped by lots of features such as obtaining details of courses, which include in their charges through the course section. Additionally, there are categorized cloud storage that carries the essential document like unmarked test files or teaching resources that are easily navigable when it is needed. Finally, yet importantly, the students are expected to derive great benefits from the application. Such as performance checking and goal setting which will illustrate subjects that learners achieved good marks or need to improve. They are also able to access group-finding features to connect with persons who share the same goals. Students can create a profile to show their aim, major, or even personality which will be also categorized in the teacher and school database if they set it publicly. This will help schools and teachers to utilize and develop every learner's strength and weak points. To sum up, the main objective of our project is to create a school system that can stand out and compete in the industry field.

    To achieve our aim, there are specific goals that we must strive for:

    • Providing user with a complete product and friendly interface. Firstly, the application must be able to perform all the functions we desired (delegating, performance checking, etc.). Secondly, the structure of the website must be intuitive. Also, the graphic, layout, colors that make the interface interactive and attractive. Finally, the product must work well on all platform and devices.
    • Possessing exceptional and convenient features. Primarily, Performance Checking will provide illustration, comparison, and recommended score that students have to achieve in their next tests (which depend on their goal setting). Furthermore, Group finding is exceptionally handy for first-year students and the one who wishes to find equal-intention partners.
    • Ensure anything in the infrastructure is stable. For instance, the cloud and storage must be available anytime for the user, the security must be strong so the users’ data would not be lost, making the application dependable and trustworthy.
    • Frequent updates through feedback. In the feedback section, every time there is any error or complaint, the team will have to check if it is rational. If it is a valid error, our team will have to work together until we figure out the way to manage it and enhance the system accordingly.

    Plan and Progress:

  • How our project began?

    • In the beginning, our group formed hastily because of our concern for the subject’s teamwork-reliant nature. Initially, we were all strangers except for Nhat Minh and Trong who studied high school in the same class. But after the first tutorial class, we decided to form a group because we share the same goals for this subject which was to achieve a Distinction grade, and because we saw that every one of us had a good work ethic.

    After the team had gotten used to each other through talking to each other in classes, the topic of our project had to be decided. This was a process that is arguably the most important as it depicts what we will spend the rest of the semester working on. We had discussions over a week to decide what the project will be about just because “IT” is such a vast topic to cover. Ultimately, we narrowed the topic down to those that are closest to us students and chose the topic of an improved learning system for students and teachers.

    The topic was chosen because we discovered certain aspects of the current learning systems in our country that could use improvements that benefit students and teachers in their path of education. For example, in most public schools the only form of education management system exists in the form of a database file which is not useful for teachers and students who want to improve their learning. And when they do want to improve their learning, they do not know how and where to improve as it often comes from the teacher’s oral or written advice which can be lost if not kept carefully. So, we decided to make this system exist online and accessible so that students and teachers can easily look for the things they want. We also saw that students who have a hard time socializing or speaking to many people harbor difficulties in finding a group, so we also aim to make that a key feature of the product.

  • How has the project progressed

    • Since the start of the project, Nhat Minh has stepped up to be the leader of our group and brining everyone’s ideas together for us to democratically pick. It has worked in our favor as far as there has been no disputes regarding the direction that the project is taking. We also setup a strict schedule of at least two online meetings per week to discuss what we did since the previous meeting, discussing new tasks to be delegated and delegating those tasks accordingly. Aside from that, a Facebook Messenger group was available as an outlet for questions regarding the tasks and short discussions between members.

    Shortly after choosing our project topic and idea, we ran into a problem while coming up with the project proposal. The main problem was making the timeline as we were unsure if we could realize the project due to our lacking skills as freshmen in the field of IT. We decided to write the timeline as if we were to build the learning management system, despite realizing the low probability of us being able to make it. However, this problem was resolved after submitting the early draft of our project proposal. Our professor commented that we should only make the timeline this way if we were to make a product. This was the first major change that we made to our project proposal; we changed the timeline so that it was realistic according to our deadlines for the assignments of the course.

    We proceed with the actual project proposal by making sure to read the rubric thoroughly before writing the individual parts. Members also frequently cross check each other’s work to make sure that no one was having any problems with parts of the proposal since everyone received the same marks for the project. After everyone was done with their part for the proposal, we had a reviewing and editing session then submitted it for grading.

    Afterwards, we had a choice between making the presentation and writing the project report. On paper, the project report is worth more and should be put to work immediately but after discussions, we decided to do an early draft of the presentation first because the project report would require a continuous stream of work and making the presentation draft quickly removes some of the workload we would have at the end of the semester when we have other subjects to worry about. Upon making the presentation, we stumbled upon a problem we have, which was the visuals for our product which we didn’t have at the time. This called for a meeting to discuss and create the design of the web-application that we envisioned in our plans. The meeting did not go to plan at first since we argued over which tool to use (Canva or Figma), but after some consideration we used Figma for the design and swiftly came up with an original design while still basing it off the current learning system we use (Canvas).

    Onto the final stretch of our group project, we started planning for the project report and how to best write it. Because it was almost the end of the semester and we had other subjects’ tests coming up as well, we had to manage our time really well in order to do this part effectively. But in the end, we finished the report in time to start working on the website.

  • How the project can be improved in the future?

    • Currently, we have only scratched the surface of our group’s idea and haven’t carried out anything yet. However, with some research into the technologies that are required for the learning management system, we can implement it and that would be the goal for our foreseeable future as well. First, we can create a framework of the website using Python and SQL. And then using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, we can make the front-end of the web-application.

    Next up, since our Scope is quite big, we aim to find out which features are most important and refine them so that the web-application can be even more user-friendly. This also helps users not be overwhelmed when using the application and make the product more appealing to users of all ages.

    Another important step to take in the future would be to test our product and have people test it. This will give us insight on how effective the platform is and how it can be improved so that we can compete with the current learning management systems like Canvas or Google Classroom.

    And finally, since people’s learning information needs to be private and we respect that privacy a lot. What we can do is research on cybersecurity and apply it to our project so that users can rely on the system keeping their information safe.

    So, with effectiveness, user-friendliness, and information security as our top values for the product, we hope that in the future we can make learning management easier for both students and teachers, which will improve the quality of education.

    Tools and Technologies

  • Programming language selection:

    • The first milestone in building a school management system is choosing the appropriate programming language for the project. This product tasked us with constructing a friendly user–interface and database used for storing data and documents from the clients. As first-year students in the field of Information Technology, deciding on the right programming language without basic knowledge in web foundation is a huge challenge

    Our research in the front–end segments results in various options. There are HTML, CSS, JavaScript, ReactJS and even Bootstrap. Each of these languages have their own superiority compared to the others which make the process of finalizing the decision even harder. In the end, our team decide to go with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript as the first two are the most feasible for beginners and JavaScript is excellent in handling web behavior.

    Regarding the back–end, there are many choices that comes to our hand, namely NodeJS, PHP, Python, .NET, Ruby, and SQL. However, we all straightforwardly unite under the idea of using Python and SQL as we believe that is the most suitable for building a database

    HTML 5:

    HTML 5 is one of the first programming languages that the students learn in their first year of university. Nevertheless, the programming language still proved to be sufficient in building a suitable user–interface. The major objective of HTML is to provide blocks that make up the structure of the website. That structure will then serve as the backbone for decoration and animation provided by CSS and JavaScript.

    CSS 3:

    Along with HTML, CSS is one of the friendliest programming languages for beginners while still being able to produce high quality interface. As the programming language is also famous nowadays, it can be executed without the risk of mismatch in the technology. The main purpose of CSS is to enable the presentation of layout, colors, and fonts.

    JavaScript:

    Although JavaScript is not initially accessible with our current knowledge, we believe it is worth the time to research and learn how to code in JavaScript. The programming language itself is one of the most popular choices regarding the Front–end section with many distinct features. In this product, JavaScript is responsible for the interactive features of web behavior.

    SQL:

    Just like JavaScript, SQL does not belong in our current knowledge asset but is tempting enough for us to strive for. It is said to possess the ability to function at high speed, meaning an efficient and quick data flow for the user. Hence, we will use SQL to construct the back–end database for the management system.

    Python:

    Being the first programming language and one of the most versatile one, Python is the first name came up to our mind when we were brainstorming our options. Famous as it is, choosing Python also give us the chance to implement many open-source library that is both highly efficient and convenient for the developer. The main logic of the product will be handled by Python.

    GitHub:

    Cooperation between freshmen in programming is challenging since everyone will have their own platform to work on and the result is conflicted when added together. That is where GitHub truly shines. By using GitHub, the delegation between team members is more feasible as they can create a branch to focus on their own part. Also, through pulling requests and committing to the main, our group can identify the conflict in our code and fix it without having to tamper the main branch.

    Risks:

    1. Lack of programming experience

      Being RMIT University first-year students, most of our team has little to no programming experience, which could be a significant roadblock to developing our project. The coding and programming process is complex and challenging, much like the school system, which is complicated and diverse. The system's foundation is built on a combination of multiple programming languages, such as Swift, SQL, or JavaScript. This is a vast area of expertise and is exorbitantly tricky for beginners. Our team can only develop a workable design for creating the system's backbone with a firm grasp of these languages. Additionally, the project will be significantly hampered and slowed down if a system mistake happens or a required method is outside our knowledge. Our group has committed to learning the programming languages on our own to lessen the harm it does and has contacted counselors who can help us.

    2. Insufficient performance monitoring

      The worst thing that could happen to any endeavor is for it to proceed more slowly than expected. It is always complex to monitor a project's completion because there are inevitably unforeseen problems. It can take longer to complete tasks because of bad experiences or poor time management, or the entire project might be placed on hold because of an unexpected error that surfaced during the trial session. Without a suitable answer, the team might have ended up meeting the deadline before completing their assignment. The additional time needed to address issues may also throw the preliminary plan off course, necessitating the members' creation of a new, time- and effort-consuming plan. To address this problem, our team created a detailed timetable with checkpoints for each stage of the project so that the participants could assess their performance considering those standards. The team will look for the issue and increase productivity to keep up with the progress if the findings are at odds with what was anticipated.

    3. Miscommunication

      Every project's success depends on effective communication. A smooth exchange of information can get the process along much more quickly. However, it is not always simple to keep the interactions between the members consistent. Teammates' varied lifestyles and work styles are significant communication barriers. For the team to interact effectively, everyone must also agree on a set location for the conversation. Without these prerequisites, a task could go unfinished due to the improper use of communication platforms, or a response to a question could be delayed and impede the project's development. To address this issue, our group decided on a set online meeting starting at 7:30 PM on Mondays and Thursdays and an offline meeting on Wednesdays. Without exception, every file and job delegation will be documented on our shared drive.

    4. Lack of dedication

      Students have occasionally abandoned projects midway through, and every group has had to take precautions against this risk for a long time. The quality of the tasks assigned to a student will undoubtedly suffer if they decide not to commit to the project, or worse, they will not even turn in the work. When this happens, the project will be hindered, and numerous changes to the plan will need to be made. Either the team will need to persuade the student to complete their task, or they will need to deal with the fact that the member will disregard that task. Dealing with the lack of commitment will always take time and effort, regardless of the outcome. To address this issue, our group's initial meeting is devoted to learning about each other's course objectives and assigning tasks accordingly. Additionally, because everyone will have agreed on the issue, they will constantly be inspired to participate in the project.

    5. Lack of engagement

      A project can be completed only when the tasks are appropriately assigned to the various team members. Nevertheless, there are cases of group members choosing not to cooperate. It is either brought on by the oddness of new friends or resistance to social interaction. Whatever the motivation, refusing to cooperate is never beneficial. The tasks cannot be completed if teammates do not work together because disagreements may occur during data investigation, design mismatches during presentations, or disputes during the evaluation of proposals. In the worst circumstances, the issues might not be resolved because of each member's ego and resistance, leading to a proposal and presentation that lacks consistency and accuracy. In week three, we had a bonding activity where we played table tennis together to solve this problem. We have also chosen a group leader who will unite all the ideas into a cohesive concept.

    6. Lack of customers' attraction

      The field of educational management applications is popular nowadays, and hence, our product has many competitors. These are big organizations with a history in the education segment, namely Canvas, Outlook, and VietSchool. To compete with those major organizations, having a refreshing user interface and optimized features is still insufficient. The project will not survive in the industry without occasional updates and significant benefits to attract consumers, as the competitors have already constructed a substantial customer royalty. To address this issue, in addition to the revolutionary changes in the current school management system, our application also needs to adapt to customers’ preferences and improve itself constantly.
